### The Problem, Made Specific

A founder in Singapore has an idea for an AI tutoring app. They Google it. They find Khanmigo, Synthesis, a dozen US competitors. They kill the idea.

**What they never saw:**

- Vietnam has 20M students, zero AI tutoring penetration, and edtech VC activity up 340% this year
- The exact model they're building just hit product-market fit in Brazil 8 months ago
- There's an ESG grant open right now specifically for SEA edtech

That information exists on the open web. It's just in Portuguese, Vietnamese, and buried in 40 different sources. No founder has time to find it. **That's Frontier.**

---

### The Three Core Modules

**1. Gap Radar** _(your arbitrage idea)_ Takes a business model → finds markets where it's proven → checks if equivalent exists in target market → scores the opportunity window. This is the flagship feature and the demo centerpiece.

**2. Signal Feed** _(real-time intel)_ Live monitoring of funding activity, regulatory changes, accelerator cohort themes, and hiring velocity across SEA + source markets. Answers: _what's hot right now, and where._

**3. Trend Bridge** _(upcoming trends)_ Surfaces emerging categories in mature markets (US, India, Brazil, Nigeria) that are 12–24 months from hitting SEA, based on VC momentum + consumer adoption curves.

---

### Why TinyFish Is The Unfair Advantage

Every module needs data that doesn't exist in any database:

- Foreign language VC blogs and local news
- App store rankings by country, live
- Accelerator portfolio pages as they update
- Job board hiring velocity across regions
- Government grant portals behind logins

This is the exact problem TinyFish solves. You're not building a prettier Crunchbase — you're building intelligence from sources Crunchbase can't touch.
